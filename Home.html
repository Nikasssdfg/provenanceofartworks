Abstract

This research is devoted to the influence of provenance of works of art on their sale at auction. The tendencies of sales of Art pieces with positive and negative, “black” provenance are characterized. The most notable examples of provenance paintings from the ASG Great Fine Arts collection. NFT art and it's relations to the provenace.

What is Provenance?

Provenance is the chronology of the ownership, custody, or location of a historical object.

Information about the ownership of a painting can come from a range of sources, including contemporary descriptions, inventories of collections, inventory numbers on the paintings themselves, and auction sale catalogs

Provenance is one of the most important factors influencing the perception of art objects. 

Research 
questions:

What are white and black provenance?

White provenance

If the artwork was previously in a famous private collection, this significantly increases its market value. 

That's what is called a positive provenance.

Black provenance

Likewise if the artwork was previously in a "bad" collection, it will decrease the value and make it harder to sell.

That's what defined as a negative provenance.

Instance:

Artworks of Peter Paul Rubens from the collection of Calouste Gulbenkian will cost more than canvases by the same authors from the collection of an unknown collector.

Instance:

If the name of Hermann Goering appears in the provenance of the artwork, it causes not only a huge price drop but also makes it nearly impossible to sell it.

CryptoPunks

CryptoPunks10,000 unique collectible characters with proof of ownership stored on the Ethereum blockchain. The project that inspired the modern CryptoArt movement. Selected press and appearances include Mashable, CNBC, The Financial Times, Bloomberg, MarketWatch, The Paris Review, Salon, The Outline, BreakerMag, Christie's of London, Art|Basel, The PBS NewsHour, The New York Times in 2018 and again in 2021. The Cryptopunks are one of the earliest examples of a "Non-Fungible Token" on Ethereum, and were inspiration for the ERC-721 standard that powers most digital art and collectibles.
